The process

Three steps, one grading

Every upload runs through the same fixed pipeline, no manual thresholds to fiddle with.

01

Upload a photo

Drop in a JPG or PNG of a fresh fruit bunch. Any angle works, but a clear, close-up shot gives the model the most to work with.

02

CNN inference

MobileNetV2 processes the raw image and outputs a probability for each of the three ripeness classes.

03

Grading + advice

You get the predicted class, a confidence score, and a plain-language harvest recommendation.

Under the hood

Why MobileNetV2 runs this app

Four architectures were benchmarked on the same dataset. The highest accuracy didn't win. Robustness to bad input did.

Baseline CNN75.36%
ResNet50V290.82%
InceptionV395.17%
MobileNetV2 (deployed)93.24%
Out-of-distribution test

Four non-FFB images, grass, banana, a hand, a random object, were run through each model to see if it would falsely commit to a ripeness class.

MobileNetV2 rejected all four, dropping below the 85% confidence threshold every time.

InceptionV3, despite the highest raw accuracy, misclassified all four at 95 to 99% confidence. ResNet50V2 passed three of four.

The three grades

What the model is looking for

Unripe

Belum Masak

Dark green to black fruitlets, firmly attached. Oil extraction is low, not ready to harvest.

Ripe

Masak

Orange-red fruitlets, some loose. Peak oil content, the optimal harvest window.

Overripe

Terlalu Masak

Dark red to purple, fruitlets detaching. FFA content is climbing, harvest immediately.
